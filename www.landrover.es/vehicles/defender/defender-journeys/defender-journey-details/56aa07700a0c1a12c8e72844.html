1984, 90 - 130

Camel Trophy - Australia

BY Camel Trophy by Land Rover 1986

Cooktown QLD 4895, Australia TO Darwin NT, Australia 3303 km

The Camel Trophy of 1986 was a daunting prospect for the fourteen international teams taking part. Beginning the expedition in Cooktown and driving across the Northern Outback was no mean feat at a total of 3,218km.

Luckily, each team was equipped with a Land Rover 90, the ideal vehicle for such treacherous conditions. One of the most challenging moments of the journey was the task of building a raft to get the vehicles across a raging river!

Despite the tough terrain and conditions, the teams surprised the organisers by arriving at their destination in Darwin after just thirteen days. The Land Rover 90s sped across the Outback to achieve the quickest race in the event’s history.
