Camel Trophy - Sulawesi

BY Camel Trophy by Land Rover 1988

Manado, Manado City, North Sulawesi, Indonesia TO Makassar, Makassar City, South Sulawesi, Indonesia 1719 km

In 1988’s Camel Trophy, only one vehicle was fit to compete against the 2,000km stretch of the untamed island of Sulawesi – the Land Rover 110, the precursor to the modern Defender.

Beginning the race in Sulawesi’s Capital City of Manago, the international teams ventured through a treacherous combination of volcanic mountains paired with dense tropical jungle. Despite the hazardous conditions, the Land Rover 110s could handle the worst that Sulawesi could throw at them.

Finally completing the journey in Ujung Pandang, the Turkish team took the trophy, however it was the Brits who won 1988’s special award for team spirit.
