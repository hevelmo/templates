2014, DEFENDER

THE GRAND ALPINE TOUR

BY RGS and Land Rover 2014

London, UK TO London, UK 4589 km

The Grand Alpine Tour was the seventh expedition to be awarded the Land Rover Bursary in partnership with the Royal Geographical Society (with IBG). The expedition carried out research on the magnitude and frequency of landslides at high altitudes to interpret and understand the changing landscape of the European Alps as a result of the changing climate.

In order to reach their research sites, in often challenging terrains along the European Alps, the team were proved with a specially modified Defender 110. The vehicle was fitted with a roof platform, incorporating a pattern, which acts as a reference point for their Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) to collect data to create 3D maps that are providing new insight into small and frequent landslides.

The team was made up of Northumbria University geographers Mark Allan, Dr Mike Lim and Thomas Shaw, who all study alpine landscapes. The route took the team from the Royal Geographical Society (with IBG) in London, UK to Trieste, Italy and along the entire length of the Alps, travelling through France, Austria, Switzerland and Slovenia. The whole journey took three months with 18,000km driven.
