Monster mash at Pikes Peak

Updated 16/6/2011

Suzuki rally legend Nobuhiro ‘Monster’ Tajima has broken the elusive 10-minute barrier with a record-break run at the world famous Pikes Peak International Hill Climb, defending his title as King of the Mountain.

Crossing the finish line in 9 minutes 51 seconds, Monster maintained his phenomenal winning streak that began in 2003.

Monster has been a fixture at the world famous event since 1988 and has now won the unlimited division a record ten times – all behind the wheel of Suzuki vehicles. This year he was behind the wheel of his SX4 race car.

After exhaustive tests in his wind facility, Monster’s 2011 SX4-based special underwent significant aerodynamic changes to adapt to the modified road surfaces for this year’s race.

And further development of his turbocharged 2.7-litre V6 engine – now measuring just over three litres’ capacity – has resulted in the SX4 special producing a phenomenal 680 kW of power and 890 Nm of torque.

The race is run on a 19.9 kilometre-long course with 156 turns that begins at an altitude of 2860 metres and finishes at the 4300 metre summit of Pikes Peak. The dramatic rise in altitude robs engines of 30 per cent of their power at the summit and competitors employ special oxygen breathing apparatus to combat the changes in air pressure.

More than 150 drivers took part in the 2011 Pikes Peak challenge, spread out among 18 classes.

Monster previously held the all-time course record with a time of 10 minutes 1.41 seconds set in 2007.
