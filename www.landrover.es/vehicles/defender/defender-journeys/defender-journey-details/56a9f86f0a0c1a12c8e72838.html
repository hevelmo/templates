SHOALS OF AGULHAS: SPIRIT OF ADVENTURE

BY Land Rover 2015

Port Elizabeth, South Africa TO Durban, South Africa 909 km

In July 2015 Land Rover, together with Global Ambassador Monty Halls, invited competition winner Brett Andersen and three friends to South Africa for the trip of a lifetime. The Shoals of Agulhas ‘Spirit of Adventure’ expedition spanned 1,000kms over 15 days and saw the crew driving across multiple terrain, swimming with sharks and discovering the true definition of adventure.

The team travelled from Port Elizabeth to Durban along the Agulhas coastline, following the route of the annual Sardine Run, a migration of sardines, which attracts thousands of marine predators and other wildlife to the same spot each year. Along the way, the expedition stopped off to help with the work carried out by Land Rover for the Born Free Foundation and met up with Land Rover Ambassador Kingsley Holgate to support his humanitarian worth with The Kingsley Foundation.

To undertake this epic journey, Monty Halls led the Land Rover convoy in his very own specially modified Marine Defender, which played a vital role in the trip. The one-off vehicle was modified to provide transport for his diving and film crew, as well as serving as a mobile basecamp and dive centre in remote, off-road locations. Packed with clever engineering and design solutions including bespoke storage for diving air tanks, a water tank and hosing facility for cleaning kit, a sliding table strong enough to bear up to 200kg and an awning to give protections from the elements the vehicle was fully fit for purpose.

A total of 10 Land Rover vehicles, plus two 15 tonne boats which were towed for the entire journey, completed the expedition. On his return, Monty Halls commented: “A great expedition, a great team, a magnificent spectacle – the memories will last a lifetime.”
