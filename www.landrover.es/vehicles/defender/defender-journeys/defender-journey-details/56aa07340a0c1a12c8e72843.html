EXPLORAR MÁS

1984, 90 - 130

Camel Trophy - Borneo

BY Camel Trophy by Land Rover 1985

Samarinda, Samarinda City, East Kalimantan, Indonesia TO Balikpapan, Balikpapan City, East Kalimantan, Indonesia 116 km

1985 was the year that saw the largest, and possibly most challenging, Camel Trophy to-date, featuring sixteen international teams in a 1,500km race across the Indonesian island of Borneo.

Equipped with a Land Rover 90, each team faced some of the harshest conditions ever experienced during the Camel Trophy. The vehicles braved mountainous jungle and swollen rivers, day in, day out. Conditions were so difficult that progress withered to just 2km per day in order to battle the varying terrain.

Nevertheless, not even the most extreme course could stop the Land Rover 90s and the German team took the trophy – plus a special award to the Brazilians for team spirit.
