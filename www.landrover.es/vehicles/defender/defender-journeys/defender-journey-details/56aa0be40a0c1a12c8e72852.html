1958, SERIES I

Housewives Himalayan Expedition

BY Housewives Choice by Land Rover 1958

London, UK TO London, UK 18494 km

In 1958, three English housewives defied convention by driving 16,000 miles in a Land Rover to the Himalayas. Land Rover provided Anne Davies, Eve Sims and Antonia Deacock with a cut-price Series I for their journey which became the trio's home for five months as they tackled Iranian deserts, Afghan mountains and chaotic Indian traffic.

As well as providing the vehicle, Land Rover helped the trio along the way with agents in most of the cities en route who met the travellers and showed them around. The group even secured an audience with the Indian prime minister, Jawaharlal Nehry, at his official residence in Delhi. He granted permission for them to travel beyond the 'inner line', which was practically unheard of. In a later interview, Davies recalled "The Himalyan Club were incredulous that we had got this permit, because lots of people had tried and had been turned down."

Davies, Sims and Deacock became the first European women to visit the remote kingdom of Zanskar and in Afghanistan they climbed a virgin mountain which they named 'Wives Peak'.
