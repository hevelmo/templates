2013, DEFENDER

Pole of Cold Expedition

BY RGS and Land Rover 2013

London, UK TO Oymyakon, Sakha Republic, Russia, 678750 12053 km

A red specially modified Land Rover Defender enabled the 'Pole of Cold' expedition to chase the onset of winter across Europe and Siberia to reach geographic researchers in the coldest place in the Northern Hemisphere, Oymyakon in north-eastern Russia, which has recorded temperatures as low as minus 67C.

The Defender was equipped with a Webasto engine heater and extra insulation around the engine bay and suspension. An extra layer of glass was fitted to the windows to create an insulating double-glazed effect and enabled the team to see through side windows whatever the conditions. In three months the Defender covered 30,000km. It The vehicle was donated loaned as part of the Land Rover's annual bursary, awarded in partnership with to the Royal Geographical Society (with IBG).
