EXPLORAR MÁS

1957, SERIES I

The Grizzly Torque

BY Foster and Bateman by Land Rover 1957

England, UK TO Australia 22970 km

After completing a master's degree in biology at the University of Toronto, Bristol Foster decided it was time for a break from education and invited his boyhood friend Robert Bateman to take a round-the-world trip in a Land Rover. The friends, with the help of Foster's father, purchased a special order Series I, which was built on May 2, 1957, in which they could pack all of their gear and live for the duration of the 60,000 kilometre trip. Land Rover provided the chassis with all running gear and front body, whilst British coach builder Pitcher, who built ambulance bodies for Land Rover, provided the custom rear body.

Ahead of the trip, Foster travelled to England for a maintenance course at the Land Rover manufacturing plant in Solihull and learnt how to completely disassemble and reassemble a Land Rover. He was joined by Bateman and they took delivery of their sand beige, square-bodied, four-wheel-drive Land Rover, with a green leather interior, sliding observation hatch and rear bunks, which they affectionately called 'Grozzly Torque'.

As they travelled across three continents, Bateman, now an internationally recognised wildlife artist, painted scenes of every country on the sides of the Land Rover. They also wrote and illustrated periodic articles to be published in the Toronto Telegraph, for which they were dubbed 'The Rover Boys'.
