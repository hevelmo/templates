1994, DEFENDER

Heading Home

BY Hedland to Perth 2015

Port Hedland WA, Australia TO Perth WA, Australia 1772 km

HOT! My wife and I took off from Port Hedland in the heat of December to get to Perth to meet up with the Indian Pacific Rail to cross the big center. We were lucky the weather was quite mild when we took off in 42 degree heat. Aircon vents worked well.

Our Landy is an old MY1994 which has seen some serious action over its lifetime. I bought it to take on adventures whilst in the Pilbara and it hasn't skipped a beat... oh well there was this one time... Anyway, it survived a hellish time in the desert for many years.

The entire drive was conducted with the windows down and enjoying the views of the Australian outback.

Port Hedland to Karratha was a good trip of approximately 240 kms. Time to settle a few bumps and rattles and fix the setup for the next day. We drove in the late afternoon and made it to Karratha quite refreshed. Landy went really well between 90 and 100 km/h. The smiles on our faces said it all, what a great adventure. It was more about enjoying the Defender in a setting rarely seen in one of the harshest environments on earth! We loved it.

In the morning it was time to be welcomed to first grade! Karratha to Carnarvon was the hottest and most grueling trip of the adventure. Some of the station owners and locals of the Pilbara outback do it every day, but, 45-48 degrees with no aircon took us back to the sixties when you burnt your bum on the vinyl seats after a day at the beach. 640 kms of red dirt and heat, took us about ten hours to complete and a ton of water. 21 year old Landy, that's had a life of being driven hard and put away wet, came through with the goods. After a day in the sun, the cool sea breezes of Carnarvon were a most welcome respite.

Carnarvon to Perth was easy. The wildlife started to appear, goats, kangaroos, wallabies, camels and donkeys were in abundance. For the majority of the trip, we seemed to be on the road by ourselves. It was the trip/adventure of a lifetime; but it won't be our last.

Good on ya Landy.
